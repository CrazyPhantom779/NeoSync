# NeoSync 1.0.0

Initial release — a NeoForge 1.21.1 port of the classic Sync experience.

## What it does

Grow extra bodies — *shells* — and switch your mind between them. Each shell has its own inventory, experience, and gamemode. Die in one and you wake up in another.

## Features

- **Shell Constructors, Shell Storages, and Treadmills** to grow, store, and power your spare bodies.
- **Cross-dimensional syncing** — your shells can live anywhere, including modded dimensions.
- **No death penalty in a shell** — dying inside a shell hands you off to another body and doesn't add to your death counter.
- **Color-code your shells** by right-clicking a Shell Storage with dye.
- **Pick how shells are chosen on death** — natural body first, nearest, or by color, all configurable.
- **Powered by redstone or energy mods** — Shell Storages accept both; Treadmills produce energy from livestock.
- **Hopper automation** for swapping equipment on stored shells.
- **Comparator output** reports either build progress or inventory fullness — toggle with the configured wrench (a stick, by default).

## Advancements

A six-step advancement tree on the **NeoSync** tab covers everything from your first Sync Core to syncing into a shell across dimensions.

## Shell appearance

Shells render with your actual Minecraft skin, captured when the shell finishes building (including slim/classic model). Change your skin later? Build a new shell to update its look.

## Mod compatibility

- **JEI** — info panels on each sync block and a *Treadmill Energy Sources* category listing every accepted mob.
- **Jade** — crosshair tooltips show owner, build progress, color, power state, and stored energy.

Both are optional. NeoSync runs fine without them.
